Google is enhancing Sign in with Google by introducing new OIDC standard claims, specifically auth_time and amr (Authentication Methods Reference), to give developers deeper session metadata. These updates let verified apps check the freshness of a user's login and the specific authentication methods used (such as MFA or hardware keys), enabling dynamic, risk-based access controls. By leveraging these federated identity signals, platforms can better prevent account takeover and fraud and implement granular policies like step-up authentication for sensitive actions.
